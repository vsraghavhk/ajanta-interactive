Ceiling Paintings

We have some excellent designs on the ceilings in this cave also. As we enter the cave we see Six Gandharva-s [nou 155] on the ceiling of the verandah. Each pair of adjoining gandharva-s have one arm common to both. A part of this can be seen in on the top portion of the colour reproduction Maanushi Buddhas and Mithuna Figures [Nou 161/162].

The entire ceiling of the main hall, Interior View of Cave 17 [kbl 157] looks like the canopy of a shamiana, the decorated wedding tent of India. An interesting geometrical pattern, a detail from this ceiling painting may be seen in Geometrical Design [asi 067]. It may also be seen that the pillars and their capitals are also decorated.

A part of the painting on the ceiling of the aisle can be seen in A View of the Aisle of Cave 17 [Nou 030], and in the scene Vishvaantara consoles his Wife [Kbl 161]. It may be seen that decorations using flowers, geometrical forms, birds and animals are painted in Squares and Rectangles - 3 [asi83] similar to the designs in Cave 1 and Cave 2.

Resting Bulls [kbl 174], a lovely study of two animals, particularly the recumbent one, of free-flowing graceful lines, painted in one such rectangle.
