Conversion of Nanda

Cave 16, 4th century, AD

Three scenes from the Conversion of Nanda of Cave 16 are painted on the left wall above doors of the first two cells of the hall. They are, Nanda shaves his Head [Kbl 150], Nanda in dejected Mood [Kbl 152] and the Dying Princess [ASI 003]. Of these the last, showing the death-scene of Nanda's newly married wife, heart-broken at Nanda's renunciation is very famous.

Nanda shaves his Head [Kbl 150] is the first scene, in which Nanda is seen sitting on the floor and a barber is shaving his head. Only the lower part of the body of the barber is visible. But a noteworthy feature of this panel is that the posture of Nanda and the inclination of his head show a keen sense of observation by the artist. A young monk is watching the scene.

We see Nanda in dejected Mood [Kbl 152] in the next scene. The tonsured Nanda is seen sitting in a pillared pavilion brooding over his pathetic condition and is filled with remorse thinking about his beautiful wife. His sitting posture is realistic, with his knee supporting his right elbow and his hand on his right cheek. The pillars are beautifully ornamented.
