Cave Sixteen

This vihaara shows the vigour and elegance of Buddhist architecture in the 5th century AD. The spacious verandah, about 20 meters long and about 3 meters wide, is flanked in front by six octagonal pillars and two pilasters. The hall, which is about 22 meters square and 4.6 meters high, has three entrances. The pilasters on both sides of the main doorway have sculptures of beautiful women standing on makara-s (crocodile-motifs). The hall contains twenty columns. The shrine, with a colossal Buddha, is scooped out from the back gallery of the hall, which has two side-chambers. The vihaara has no antechamber.

An interesting feature of this cave is that its plan is incised in an inscription on the left-hand wall near the end of the facade.

Sculptures: The front aisle has its ceiling carved in imitation of wooden rafters and beams. Carved on the bracket-capitals are some of the fine gandharva-sculptures, perhaps intended to convey the effect of a celestial region. These figures - dwarfs, musicians and flying couples - are shown in varying moods, a few groaning under weight, some serious, others taking it lightly and still others enjoying it. Specially interesting are the two vidyadhara-s (flying couples) notable for their graceful poise and features.

Paintings: There are a number of paintings that have survived the ravages of time and vandalism. The following have been taken for detailed discussion.

Maho-sadha and Four envious Counsellors [Mjt 111], a scene from Mahaa-Ummaga Jaataka, the story of the supernatural child Maho-sadha, is painted on the left wall of the front corridor, and is continued on the adjoining pilaster.

The story of the Conversion of Nanda is narrated in this cave also, and more forcefully than in Cave 1. It is painted in the left corridor, above the doors of the first and second cells from the left. The scene in which Nanda shaves his Head [Kbl 150] is a realistic portrayal, and Nanda in dejected Mood [Kbl 152] is full of pathos. But the most important scene and one of the best compositions of Ajanta is Dying Princess [ASI 003]. Several scenes from this episode are found in this cave and are worth studying in detail.
