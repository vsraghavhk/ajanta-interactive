Mahaa-hamsa Jaataka

Cave 2, 5th Century AD

There are four scenes depicted in Mahaa-hamsa Jaataka of Cave 2. The story is painted on the left wall of the front verandah. The lotus scenes are on either side of the cell door. The palace scene is above the cell door.

That the Ajanta artist was conversant with both normal perspective and reverse perspective was mentioned elsewhere. It was also pointed out that the reverse perspective was employed in the scene Dancing Girl with Musicians [Kbl 091]. We shall come across the reverse perspective in the Game of Dice [Kbl 132], a scene from Vidhura-pandita Jaataka painted in this cave.

In this panel, Gate in King Brahma-datta’s Palace [Mjt 097], the artist has employed the normal perspective. The colouring and the shading bring out clearly the form, shape and geometry of the gate and its environs. Anther example of the use of normal perspective is the panel Monastery [Kbl 227R], a scene from Shibi Jaataka of Cave 17.
