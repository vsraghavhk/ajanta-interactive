Chaitya Interior [Nou 25]: Chaitya interior is an architectural and sculptural treasure, with the lavish sculptural decoration on pillars, capital and triforium and a standing image of the Buddha against the stoopa within a highly ornamented niche. On the capitals and the triforium are niches containing Buddha figures in dhyana mudra (meditating-attitude) separated from each other by a variety of foliage designs. The stoopa itself attracts the visitor and holds him spellbound by its artistic charm. In contrast to the stoopa-s in caves 9 and 10, this stoopa shows advanced characteristic with base-mouldings, a round anda and a tall harmika with tapering triple chhatravali-s supported by yaksha-s and surmounted by a poorna ghata (ceremonial-jar), reaching to the vaulted ceiling. The serenity and charm of the central Buddha dominate the warm spiritual atmosphere of the prayer-chamber.
