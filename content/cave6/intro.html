Cave Six

This cave is the only two-storied cave in Ajanta, whereas in Ellora one may find quite a few. This cave belongs to the 5th-6th centuries AD. The doorway is lavishly carved and the lower floor main hall has sixteen simple pillars without capitals. The shrine approached through an ante-chamber houses an image of the Buddha sitting on a lion throne.

To the right of the entrance is the staircase leading to the upper floor. The main hall of the upper floor has twelve pillars and two chapels on either side. An impressive figure of the Buddha is carved in the shrine approachable through an ante-chamber. The deity is attended by flying apsara-s and attendants.

Like all the other caves the walls of the ground and upper floors must have been painted, of which very little remains.

The following are some of the surviving paintings.

Bhikshu with Lotus [Kbl 024], painted near the feet of the Buddha in the Niche [Kbl 022], on the left wall of the ante-chamber of the first floor, is an excellent portraiture.

The miracle performed by the Buddha at Shraavasti to baffle his critics is depicted in this cave also. It may be seen on the right-hand wall of the chapel in the front corridor of the upper storey and a part is shown in Buddha baffles Critics [Kbl 023].
