Bhikshu with Lotus

Cave 6, Late 5th century AD

Bikshu with Lotus [Kbl 024], painted near the feet of the Buddha in the Niche [Kbl 022], on the left wall of the ante-chamber of the first floor, is an excellent portraiture.

Here is an example of proper balance between sculpture and painting, a special feature of Indian art. On each sidewall of the ante-chamber in the first floor are elaborately sculpted images of the Buddha. The painting on all of them had vanished as in the other sculptures in Ajanta. But traces of the same can be noticed here and there. The main image is the Buddha in the Niche [Kbl 022]. At His feet is painted the Bhikshu with Lotus [Kbl 024].

The bhikshu is kneeling before the image of the Buddha. On the left-hand he is holding aloft a bunch of three lotuses and a metal incense burner on his right-hand. The three lotuses perhaps signify the ‘three jewels’ (tri-ratna), the Buddha, the Order and the Law. The kneeling posture and the expression on his face amply portrays devotion, dedication and humility.

The choice of this painting is also to show the varying techniques the Ajanta artist employed. The lines, thick or thin based on the pressure on the brush, depends on the theme and placement. Even the colour of the lines has significance. This figure is drawn in a firm black outline and the artist has employed only two tones for the figure, apart from the background. Devotee with Lotus [Mjt 146] is a similar painting from Cave 2, belonging to the same period.
