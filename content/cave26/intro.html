Cave Twenty-six

This chaitya follows cave 19 in general arrangement and decoration, but is considerably larger. This cave is also taken for discussion for its sculptural details, and the variations in approach to sculptural decoration.

In this cave the departure from the earlier tradition of decorating the interior walls with painting has been replaced by a large number of small and large sculptures panels. However, the ornamentation, executed in meticulous detail, gives the feeling of excess and a lack of the graceful proportions and rhythmic correlation of Cave 19.

Mention may also be made on changes in the structure and the decoration of the stoopa from the earlier ones. The stoopa proper has been relegated to the background, the emphasis being more on the elongated and decorated plinth. In the front of it is carved with a figure of the Buddha seated in the pralamba paada (hanging-legs) attitude under a pavilion.

There are two sculptural compositions, which arrest our attention. Both of them are found on the left wall. One is a colossal reclining Buddha depicting His Mahaa-parinirvaana [Nou 215]. The other is Temptations of the Buddha - Sculpture (Line ?), a composition in painting of the same theme, Temptations of the Buddha [Nou 048] has been painted in Cave 1, a comparison between that and this sculpture was made while discussing the painting.
